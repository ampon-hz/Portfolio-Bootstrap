"THE SILENCER" 3-SHOT FILM (2022)

Directed by Hazel Ampon
Screenplay by Hazel Ampon, Jazmine Sta Maria & Carl Joshua Aquino
Cinematography by Carl Joshua Aquino
Edited by Carl Joshua Aquino and Ann Chealzee Nerida







DIRECTOR'S VISION


In light of the events regarding unjust killings of journalists in the Philippines, this film was created as a form of outcry and a protest. Directing and developing the story for “The Silencer” was my own means of taking a stand through artistic expression, in hopes of starting conversations and inspiring viewers to speak up against injustice.

Every event in the film unfolded as the camera showed a 360 shot of the main character’s living area. Because of this, post production techniques were quite limited. Keyframes were used to zoom in on the two characters during the point of rising action and climax, while color grading was utilized to set a colder, more serious atmosphere particularly at the near end of the film. The striking red color of the plastic bag was greatly enhanced to emphasize its symbolism. Transitions were mainly used to cut the 360 shots shorter, in accordance with the total running time of one minute. Lastly, a fitting background music was used to bring a feeling of rising tension.
